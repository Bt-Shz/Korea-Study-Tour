In the afternoon of May 19th, 2026, our group visited the **Graduate School of AI at KAIST**, housed at the AI Hub in Yangjae, Seoul. KAIST is widely regarded as Korea's premier institute of science and technology, and its dedicated graduate school of artificial intelligence is one of the most active AI research centres in the country.

After the morning at SKKU, all participants travelled from Suwon toward Seoul via Suwon and Jeongja, alighting at Yangjae Citizen Forest and walking the last stretch to the AI Hub, with lunch along the way.

## The visit

We were welcomed on the 6th floor by **Prof. Kate Shim**, who opened the afternoon with an introduction to the department before turning to her own group, the **CVML (Computer Vision & Machine Learning) Lab**. She walked us through the lab's research interests and recent topics, giving us a clear picture of the questions her students are pursuing in computer vision and learning.

The highlight was a casual meeting with the CVML graduate students themselves. In a relaxed, conversational setting they shared their projects and answered our questions, and then took us on a tour of the research space where the work actually happens.

## Programme

- **2:00 PM** — Introduction to the department (15–20 min)
- **2:20 PM** — CVML Lab's research interests and recent topics (40 min)
- **3:00 PM** — Casual meeting with CVML graduate students, Q&A, and a tour of the research space (60 min)

## Our hosts

- **Prof. Kate Shim** — Graduate School of AI at KAIST, CVML Lab
