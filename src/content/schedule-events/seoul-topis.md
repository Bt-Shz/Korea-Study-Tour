For the afternoon, we regrouped at **1:00 PM** to visit **Seoul TOPIS** (Seoul Transport Operation and Information Service) — the control centre that watches over and manages transport across the entire city, all from the 5th floor of City Hall.

Once everyone was back together after the morning's free time, we got a guided introduction to the centre. They showed us how TOPIS pulls together live data from buses, the metro, taxis and roads onto a huge wall of screens, and how the operators turn that flood of information into clear, real-time guidance for the public.

## What we learned

- How Seoul gathers and ties together transport data from across the whole city.
- How that big data gets processed and visualised for operators and citizens alike.
- How a smart-city control centre spots incidents and keeps traffic moving.

For a group of computer-science students, TOPIS was the perfect finale — a living, working example of data engineering and AI running at the scale of a megacity, tying together everything we'd seen all week into one system that millions of people lean on every single day.

And with that, the visit wrapped up and we were dismissed for the afternoon.
