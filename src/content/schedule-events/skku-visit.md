On May 19th, 2026, our group had the opportunity to visit **Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU)**, one of the oldest institutions in East Asia. Tracing its roots back to 1398 as the foremost Confucian academy of the Joseon dynasty, SKKU today pairs that long heritage with one of Korea's most ambitious engineering and science programmes on its Natural Sciences Campus in Suwon.

After gathering at our hotels in the morning, all participants walked to Sinchon Station together and took the Seoul Metro through the busy Sindorim interchange toward SKKU Station, then walked up to the Engineering Building through the campus gate, arriving in time for the 10:00 AM session.

## The visit

We were warmly received by **Prof. Kyuhong Shim** and **Prof. Jongwuk Lee**, who each introduced their research interests and recent work. The professors walked us through how their groups approach machine learning and artificial intelligence, sharing the kinds of problems their students are currently tackling and the conferences where their work appears. Throughout the morning there was time for questions, and the conversation moved easily between research, graduate study, and student life at SKKU.

Afterwards, our hosts guided us through the engineering department, pointing out the laboratories and facilities where much of that research takes place and giving us a feel for the day-to-day rhythm of a Korean engineering campus.

## What we saw

- An introduction to the professors' research interests and recent publications.
- A walk through the engineering department and its research laboratories.
- An open discussion on research directions, graduate admissions, and student life.

## Our hosts

- **Prof. Kyuhong Shim** — Sungkyunkwan University
- **Prof. Jongwuk Lee** — Sungkyunkwan University

With the morning session complete, the group set off for the Graduate School of AI at KAIST, with lunch along the way.
