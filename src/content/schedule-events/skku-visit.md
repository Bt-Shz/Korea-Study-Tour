Day two started bright and early, and our first stop was **Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU)** — one of the oldest institutions in all of East Asia. Its roots reach back to **1398**, when it was the leading Confucian academy of the Joseon dynasty, and today it blends that deep history with one of Korea's most ambitious engineering and science programmes out on its Natural Sciences Campus in Suwon.

Getting there was half the adventure. After meeting up at our hotels, we walked over to Sinchon Station together and rode the metro out toward Suwon, squeezing through the famously hectic **Sindorim** interchange along the way. We surfaced at SKKU Station and made the short climb up to the **Engineering Building**, arriving just in time for the 10:00 session.

## The visit

We were warmly welcomed by **Prof. Kyuhong Shim** and **Prof. Jongwuk Lee**, who each walked us through their research and the kinds of problems their students are tackling right now — from machine learning to the latest ideas in AI. The mood stayed relaxed, with plenty of room for questions, and the conversation drifted easily between research, what graduate study is actually like, and student life on campus.

Afterwards, our hosts showed us around the engineering department and its labs, giving us a real feel for the day-to-day rhythm of a Korean engineering campus.
